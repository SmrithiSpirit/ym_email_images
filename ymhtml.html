Et tu rolling out lengthy forms, Brute?

  

Lengthy forms do crush the interest. Any contemporary form typically has two parts:

  • Asking the users what are they looking for
  • Asking them about their personal contact info

The personal contacts part

  • increases the form-filling time
  • Unpardonably bores the users to death (Let's not do a Brutus)

  

(More bitter, such questions churn the potential leads out. Alas!)

       
  

Thou shalt be blessed with pre-filled forms :innocent:

  

You run ads on Facebook, don't you? The FB users instantly find value while checking your ad. So far so good.
Now, the click-to-ad redirects to …..oh goshhhh a form.

The BIGGEST challenge now is to successfully collect the lead. How, how, how?

Of course, with FB's god-sent feature of Pre-filled forms.

  
               

With pre-filled forms

  • the personal contact info is auto-populated thereby saving time
  • the brands can ask only the important user requirements
  • the drop-off rate reduces eventually capturing more leads
  
     
       
               

Caesar is now saved. Can we chat with him?

Great. The users are successfully saved from long forms. What next? Your brand can now contextually take the conversation on chatbot.
     
       

The chatbot can:


  • acknowledge the form submission
  • gently showcase offerings based on form submission
  • help the users in booking a sales appointment and such
